About Me

I'm a passionate and self-taught programmer, now a Master's student in Media Computer Science at the University of Vienna. My journey into programming began long before my formal education, driven by curiosity and a deep interest in creating, learning, and solving real-world problems through code.

During my Bachelor's, I explored a wide range of programming areas, with a special focus on media computer science. I took courses on platforms like edX and Udemy, expanding my knowledge beyond the classroom. My Bachelor's thesis was a deep dive into image generation and classification using Unity and TensorFlow.

During one summer, I had the opportunity to intern at Partium, where I worked as part of their R&D team. There, I applied the skills from my thesis, focusing on software engineering for innovative solutions. This experience led to an offer for a junior position, which I accepted. Simultaneously, I began my Master's, further honing my skills in game development, digital media, and computer graphics.

Currently, I'm working on my Master's thesis, which involves building a procedural planet generation system and LOD (Level of Detail) algorithm to enable realistic and immersive world exploration. This project reflects my core passion for game development and computer graphics.

Beyond academics, I love learning new things—whether it's building my own game engine, creating small tools to automate tasks, or experimenting with different data-driven projects. I'm also deeply involved in game modding, working on a Super Mario 64 ROM hack to push my creativity and technical skills.

At the heart of everything I do is a drive to learn and create. Whether it's developing a new skill, exploring a new technology, or diving into a complex problem, I always aim to stay curious and push the boundaries of what's possible.

Key Interests: Game Development, Computer Graphics, Machine Learning, Software Engineering
