About Me

I'm a passionate and self-taught programmer and currently Master student in Media Computer Science at the University of Vienna. My journey into programming began long before my formal education, driven by curiosity and a deep interest in creating, learning, and solving real-world problems through code.

During my Bachelor, I explored a wide range of programming areas, with a special focus on media computer science. I took courses on platforms like edX and Udemy, expanding my knowledge beyond the classroom. My Bachelor's thesis was a deep dive into image generation and classification using Unity and TensorFlow.

During summer 2022, I had the opportunity to intern at Partium, where I worked as part of their R&D team. There, I applied the skills from my thesis and university overall, focusing on software engineering for innovative solutions. This experience led to an offer for a junior position, which I accepted. Simultaneously, I began my Master, further improving my skills in game development, computer vision, and computer graphics.

Currently, I'm working on my Master's thesis, which involves building a procedural planet generation system and LOD (Level of Detail) algorithm to enable realistic and immersive world exploration. This project reflects my core passion for game development and computer graphics. Additionally, I am currently transforming my thesis into a paper for the International Conference on Entertainment Computing and was able to present my work to other people. This experience intensified my passion for research and its potential impact.
